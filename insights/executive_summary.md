# Executive Summary

The platform is growing, but order volume alone is not enough to judge performance. Profit quality varies by city, cuisine, customer tenure, and discount level.

Discounts appear to support repeat behaviour in some cases, especially with newer customers, but high discounts often reduce margin without creating a clear enough retention benefit. The company should target discounts more carefully rather than using broad promotions.

Delivery reliability is a clear business issue. Delayed orders have lower ratings on average, and certain city-zone combinations show repeated delay pressure. These areas should be treated as operational priorities before more marketing spend is added.

The strongest opportunity is to combine commercial and operations action: protect high-profit customer groups, reduce poor-quality discounting, and fix delivery issues in zones where ratings and repeat behaviour are at risk.
