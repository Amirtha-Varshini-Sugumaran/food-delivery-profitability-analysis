# Business Insights

## Executive Read

The platform is generating meaningful order volume, but the analysis shows that growth quality is uneven. Profitability, discount efficiency, delivery reliability, and repeat behaviour vary across cities, zones, cuisines, and customer groups.

The strongest business signal is that the company should not manage growth using order count alone. Orders need to be reviewed alongside gross profit, discount cost, delay rate, rating, and repeat behaviour.

## 1. Profitability Is Concentrated

Some cities and cuisine groups contribute stronger gross profit, while others rely more heavily on discounts or show weaker margins. This means a simple “top cities by orders” view can be misleading.

Commercial teams should focus on profit per order and margin percentage, not just total order value. A high-volume segment can still be unattractive if discounts and refunds absorb too much margin.

## 2. Discounts Are Not Equally Productive

Discounts appear useful for customer activation, especially for newer customers, but higher discount bands do not automatically create healthy repeat behaviour.

The analysis shows why discounting needs tighter control:

- Some customers repeat only when heavily discounted.
- High discounts can reduce profit per order even when repeat rate improves.
- Discount effectiveness differs by acquisition channel and tenure group.

The business should measure discount success using repeat rate and profit together.

## 3. Delivery Delays Affect Customer Experience

Delayed orders have lower average ratings than on-time orders. This makes delivery performance a customer retention issue, not just an operations metric.

The issue is not evenly spread. Certain city-zone combinations show higher delay rates and lower ratings, which suggests operational pressure in specific local areas.

The most useful view is not only “which city is delayed,” but “which city-zone-restaurant combinations repeatedly create poor customer experience.”

## 4. Restaurant-Zone Performance Matters

Restaurant-level analysis alone hides useful detail. A restaurant can perform well overall but still create problems in specific zones because of distance, preparation time, weather, or local delivery capacity.

For operational action, the best unit of analysis is restaurant plus zone. This helps the business decide where to adjust promise times, driver coverage, or restaurant expectations.

## 5. Retention Depends On Value And Service

Repeat behaviour is influenced by more than discounts. Customer tenure, rating, delivery experience, acquisition channel, and margin all matter.

The customer segmentation separates customers into practical groups:

- high-value loyal customers
- high-profit occasional customers
- high-volume low-margin customers
- standard customers

This helps the business avoid treating all repeat customers as equally valuable.

## Key Takeaways

- Order growth should be judged against profit and delivery reliability.
- Discount strategy should be targeted, not broad.
- Delay hotspots should be fixed before adding more demand through campaigns.
- Restaurant-zone performance gives operations a more useful action view.
- Retention activity should prioritise profitable customers, not only frequent customers.
