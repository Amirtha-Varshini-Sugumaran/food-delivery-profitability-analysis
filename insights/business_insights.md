# Business Insights

## 1. Profit Is Not Evenly Spread

High order count does not always mean strong margin. Some frequent customers are expensive to retain because they rely heavily on discounts.

## 2. Discounts Need More Control

Discounts are useful for activation, especially with newer customers, but high discount bands do not always produce enough repeat behaviour to justify the cost.

## 3. Delays Are A Retention Risk

Delayed deliveries are associated with lower ratings. This matters because ratings are one of the clearest customer signals in the dataset.

## 4. Restaurant-Zone Combinations Matter

Restaurant performance should not be reviewed only at restaurant level. Restaurant plus zone is a better operational view because it combines demand, distance, and local delivery pressure.

## 5. Retention Is A Mix Of Value And Experience

Repeat behaviour is not explained by discounts alone. Tenure, rating, delay experience, acquisition channel, and profit quality all matter.
