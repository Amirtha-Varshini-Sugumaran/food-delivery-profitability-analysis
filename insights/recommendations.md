# Recommendations

## Priority 1: Fix Delivery Hotspots Before Adding Demand

Focus first on city-zone combinations with high delay rates and lower ratings.

Recommended actions:

- Review driver coverage in the worst-performing zones.
- Adjust promised delivery times during rain, weekends, and festival periods.
- Flag restaurant-zone combinations where delays repeat across multiple orders.
- Track delay rate and average rating together in weekly operations reviews.

Business value:

Reducing delays should improve customer experience and protect repeat behaviour, especially in zones where ratings are already under pressure.

## Priority 2: Tighten Discount Strategy

Move away from broad high-discount campaigns. Discounts should be used where they protect or create profitable repeat behaviour.

Recommended actions:

- Cap high discounts for customers who repeatedly generate low-margin orders.
- Use smaller targeted offers for customers with good margin but lower recent activity.
- Compare repeat lift against discount cost by acquisition channel.
- Review discount performance by customer tenure group.

Business value:

This protects margin while still allowing the growth team to use offers where they are commercially useful.

## Priority 3: Prioritise Profitable Customer Retention

Not all repeat customers are equally valuable. The business should prioritise high-profit loyal customers and high-profit occasional customers before spending heavily on low-margin frequent users.

Recommended actions:

- Create customer segments based on order frequency, gross profit, margin, and delay experience.
- Monitor high-value customers who recently had delayed or low-rated orders.
- Use retention campaigns for customers where the expected margin supports the offer.

Business value:

Retention spend becomes more focused and less dependent on blanket discounts.

## Priority 4: Improve Restaurant-Zone Management

Restaurant performance should be reviewed at restaurant-zone level, not only restaurant level.

Recommended actions:

- Identify restaurants with strong demand but repeated zone-level delays.
- Share delay and rating feedback with restaurant partners.
- Review preparation time assumptions for restaurants with repeated late deliveries.
- Consider temporary throttling or adjusted promise times during peak demand.

Business value:

This gives operations and partner managers a clearer way to fix specific service problems.

## Priority 5: Use The Repeat Score As A Decision Support Tool

The repeat-order scorecard should support prioritisation, not replace business judgement.

Recommended actions:

- Use repeat likelihood alongside gross profit and discount cost.
- Avoid targeting customers who are likely to repeat but consistently low-margin.
- Refresh the model after new campaign, rating, and delivery data is available.

Business value:

The model helps focus attention, but the final decision should still consider profitability and customer experience.

## Recommended Next Steps

1. Build the Power BI dashboard using the cleaned dataset and KPI measures.
2. Review delay hotspots with operations and restaurant partner teams.
3. Run a controlled discount test by tenure group and acquisition channel.
4. Track whether improved delivery performance increases rating and repeat behaviour.
